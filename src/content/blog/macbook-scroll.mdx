Product screenshots are essential, but they are also boring. A static image on a white background does not inspire confidence. The MacBook Scroll component turns your screenshot into a reveal moment, letting users scroll into the product experience rather than just looking at a picture of it.

## What it does

MacBook Scroll shows a 3D-perspective laptop that starts closed at the top of the scroll trigger zone. As the user scrolls down, the lid opens progressively and reveals a screenshot inside the display. By the time the laptop is fully open, the screenshot is fully visible and the product speaks for itself.

## How it works

The component uses an Intersection Observer combined with a scroll event listener to calculate how far through the trigger zone the user has scrolled, expressed as a value from 0 to 1. This progress value drives the lid rotation angle (from closed to open) via a CSS custom property applied to a 3D-transformed element.

The screenshot fades in as the lid approaches its fully open position, timed to match the animation so the reveal feels intentional.

## Key properties

- **screenshot** - URL of the image to display inside the laptop screen
- **scrollRange** - Height of the scroll trigger zone in pixels (default: 800)
- **lidAngle** - Maximum opening angle in degrees (default: 135)
- **perspective** - CSS perspective value for the 3D scene

## When to use it

This is the go-to component for SaaS landing pages where showing the product in context is important. Place it after the hero with a strong heading above it and the product screenshot inside.

Make sure the screenshot is high resolution. It will be displayed prominently and a blurry image will undercut the whole effect.
