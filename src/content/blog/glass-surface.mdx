Frosted glass is one of those effects that looks effortless when done right and terrible when done halfway. Getting it right is mostly about controlling the context it appears in.

## What it does

The Glass Surface component provides a frosted glass panel as a reusable primitive. It handles backdrop blur, a subtle tint, and a thin edge border that catches light, so you get consistent glass material across your UI without re-implementing it everywhere.

## How it works

The component combines `backdrop-filter: blur()` with a semi-transparent background color. A 1px border with a translucent white or light color on the top and left edges creates the illusion of a polished surface catching light from above.

The implementation is intentionally simple because the effect is purely CSS. The component mainly provides consistent prop-driven control over blur intensity and tint.

## Key properties

- **blur** - Blur radius in pixels
- **tint** - Background color with opacity (e.g., rgba(255,255,255,0.1))
- **border** - Show edge highlight border (default: true)
- **borderRadius** - Corner radius
- **padding** - Internal padding for content

## When to use it

Use this as the base for cards, modals, tooltips, or navigation bars that float over a rich background. It works best over images, videos, or colorful gradients where the blur reveals something worth blurring.

On dark backgrounds, use a white tint. On light backgrounds, use a near-black tint. The contrast is what makes glass legible.
