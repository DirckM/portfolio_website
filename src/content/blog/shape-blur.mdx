The best backgrounds don't compete with your content. They breathe beneath it. Shape Blur creates slow-moving, heavily blurred organic shapes that shift and morph, giving any section a sense of ambient depth without ever grabbing focus.

## What it does

Soft, rounded shapes drift across the canvas, morphing between forms. Everything is rendered with a heavy blur pass, so the result is closer to a painted wash of color than recognizable geometry. The motion is slow and continuous, never repeating exactly.

## How it works

Three.js renders a small set of mesh objects using morph target animation to shift their geometry over time. A custom post-processing pass applies a large-radius Gaussian blur to the entire scene output.

Each shape has its own randomized velocity and morph speed. The camera is fixed and orthographic so the shapes feel 2D despite being 3D geometry.

## Key properties

- **shapeCount** - Number of shapes in the scene (default: 5)
- **blurRadius** - Size of the Gaussian blur kernel (default: 80px)
- **speed** - Base drift speed (default: 0.3)
- **colors** - Array of colors assigned randomly to shapes
- **opacity** - Global opacity of the canvas (default: 0.6)
- **morphSpeed** - How fast shapes change form (default: 0.5)

## When to use it

This is a background treatment, not a foreground element. Layer it behind hero text, feature sections, or pricing cards to add warmth and motion without distraction.

Limit colors to two or three from a cohesive palette. Too many colors start to look chaotic once blurred together.
