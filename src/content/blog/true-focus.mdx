Reading comprehension tools have known for a while that isolating words helps with focus. True Focus borrows that idea and turns it into a UI animation. Every word is blurred out except the one in the spotlight. It is striking, and oddly meditative.

## What it does

True Focus splits a sentence into words and blurs all of them except the currently active word. The focus steps through each word on a timer, or responds to hover, creating a spotlight that travels across the text.

## How it works

The component splits the string by spaces and renders each word as a `motion.span`. Non-focused words have a CSS `filter: blur(4px)` and reduced `opacity`. The focused word is sharp and fully opaque. Framer Motion handles the transition between states smoothly.

An internal timer or scroll/hover trigger advances the focus index through the array. The transition is fast enough to feel responsive but slow enough to be readable.

## Key properties

- **text** - The sentence to display
- **mode** - Auto-advance timer, hover, or click to move focus
- **interval** - Time between word transitions in auto mode (ms)
- **blurAmount** - Strength of blur on unfocused words (px)
- **animationDuration** - Speed of the focus transition

## When to use it

Use this for taglines, manifesto statements, or any short declaration you want people to actually read word by word. It is also a surprisingly effective way to animate a list of skills or values without a standard carousel.

Keep blur around 4-6px. Heavier blur makes it hard to perceive the surrounding words at all, which kills the context.
