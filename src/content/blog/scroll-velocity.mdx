Most marquees scroll at a fixed pace regardless of what you do. That feels mechanical. A scroll velocity marquee responds to you: scroll fast and it races, slow down and it coasts. That responsiveness makes it feel alive in a way a static ticker never does.

## What it does

Scroll Velocity renders a looping text marquee that ties its playback speed to the user's scroll velocity. Faster scrolling accelerates the marquee; stopping or reversing slows it back to the base speed. Scroll up and it can even reverse direction.

## How it works

Framer Motion's `useScroll` and `useVelocity` hooks capture the scroll position and its derivative. The velocity value feeds into `useTransform` to produce an offset multiplier, which is applied to the marquee's `x` translation. `useSpring` smooths the velocity so the marquee does not snap erratically.

The marquee itself duplicates the text string enough times to fill the viewport width and then loops the translation seamlessly.

## Key properties

- **text** - The string to repeat in the marquee
- **baseVelocity** - Default scroll speed when not actively scrolling
- **velocityFactor** - How strongly scroll speed affects marquee speed
- **direction** - Default direction (1 for left, -1 for right)
- **className** - For font size, color, and spacing overrides

## When to use it

This works beautifully as a separator between sections on a long scroll page. It is also great in hero sections where it runs beneath or around the main content. Keep the text short and repeating, like a brand name, a tagline, or a list of services.

A `baseVelocity` of 2-4 combined with a `velocityFactor` of 3-5 gives a satisfying snap when scrolling fast.
