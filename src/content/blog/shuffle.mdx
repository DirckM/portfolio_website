Imagine text that does not know what it wants to say yet. It cycles through random characters, flickers, then locks into place. It looks like something decoding, something arriving from a signal. That energy is exactly what this component delivers.

## What it does

The Shuffle component animates each character of a string through a rapid series of random characters before settling on the correct one. The final text resolves character by character, left to right, giving the impression of decryption or live rendering.

## How it works

Each character slot runs its own interval using `setInterval`, pulling from a pool of random alphanumeric characters. After a set number of cycles, the slot locks to the correct character and the interval clears. A stagger offset means slots resolve in order rather than all at once.

The randomness pool can be customized to change the aesthetic from alphanumeric to symbols or even emoji-free characters.

## Key properties

- **text** - The final string to resolve to
- **speed** - How fast characters cycle (lower is faster)
- **revealDelay** - Stagger delay between each character resolving
- **characters** - Pool of random characters to cycle through
- **trigger** - What starts the animation: mount, hover, or scroll into view

## When to use it

This works brilliantly for tech-forward brands, data-heavy dashboards, or anything that wants a "loading from a server" vibe. Use it on short labels or headings, not paragraphs. The effect loses its impact at length.

Keep the cycle speed fast (10-30ms) and the stagger tight (30-50ms) so the whole animation completes in under a second.
